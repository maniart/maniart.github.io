Muslim Sufi Mystics

Borrowing heavily from Jewish mysitcism, they continue on the tradition of the Human Prototype.

Muslim philosophers, Sufi mystics and their hermeneutics of Qoran vs. verbatim traditionalism. They reject the duality of the prototype proposed by Philo, and brings mand and god even one step closer. [code, diagram] Sufi mystics "read between the lines", and derive a different narrative. They dismiss the eternal sin, and redefine the man-god relationship as a rather horizontal one: It is not a journey of repent and attonment, but one of passion, longing and a quest for a reunion. In their worldview, they elevate man from a sinner's place to a heroic explorer looking for their way back home.

Muslim Sufi Mystics

This belief system is farther removed from the verbatim Quranic text, and delves deeper into the realm of hermeneutics. It sees the prototype of Human at the highest elevations in heaven, since it was the only being curious and brave enough to do the unthinkable. In this scenario, man does not "fall" from heaven, but rather embarks upon a long journey that only he, the most gifted and brave of beings can handle, and he will return home, because he is created from God himself, with no intermediary objects.

وَنَفَخْتُ فِيهِ مِنْ رُوحِي

And I blew in him from my own soul

Quran, سورة ص
